The torque page



Have you ever tried turning a bolt with a wrench and discovered that it's easier to push down when you are further away from the bolt? Well thats called torque!
What is Torque?
Torque (τ) measures how much a force causes an object to rotate. It depends on two things:
How strong the force is.
How far the force is applied from the rotation point (pivot).
Direction of Torque Torque acts in a direction that’s perpendicular to both the distance from the pivot and the force itself. You can find this direction using the right-hand rule. Units The unit of torque is the newton-meter (N·m). Basic Formula The basic formula for torque is: 𝜏 = 𝐹 × 𝑟 τ=F×r where:
𝜏 τ is the torque. 𝐹 F is the force applied. 𝑟 r is the distance from the pivot to where the force is applied. If the force is applied at a right angle (90°) to the distance: 𝜏 = 𝑟 × 𝐹 τ=r×F
Example
Imagine a door that’s 1.5 meters wide. If you push on the edge with a force of 20 newtons, here’s how you calculate the torque:
Distance (r) = 1.5 meters Force (F) = 20 newtons Angle (θ) = 90° (since the force is perpendicular) Using the simplified formula: 𝜏 = 𝑟 × 𝐹 = 1.5 m × 20 N = 30 N m τ=r×F=1.5 m×20 N=30 N Result: The torque applied to the door is 30 N·m.
