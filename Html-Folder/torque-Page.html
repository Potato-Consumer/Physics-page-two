The torque page



Have you ever tried turning a bolt with a wrench and discovered that it's easier to push down when you are further away from the bolt? Well thats called torque! Main Principles Definition of Torque: Torque (τ) is the measure of the force causing an object to rotate. It depends on both the magnitude of the force applied and the distance from the axis of rotation to where the force is applied. Direction: Torque has a direction, which is perpendicular to the plane formed by the radius vector and the force vector. The direction is given by the right-hand rule. Units: The SI unit of torque is the newton-meter (N·m). You apply a force of 20 newtons perpendicular to the door at the edge (farthest from the hinges). Calculate the torque applied to the door.
